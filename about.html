About Us

Learn about our mission, values, and the people behind CDAC

Our Story

The Carolina Digital Access Coalition was founded in 2025 when we noticed a troubling pattern: schools, businesses, and organizations were routinely discarding perfectly functional computer equipment during upgrade cycles. Meanwhile, students, low-income families, and community organizations were struggling without access to basic computing technology.

What started as a small effort to refurbish a few donated laptops has grown into a regional initiative serving communities across the Carolinas. We've built partnerships with schools, businesses, recycling centers, and community organizations to create a sustainable pipeline that keeps functional hardware out of landfills and in the hands of people who need it.

Our Mission

The Carolina Digital Access Coalition refurbishes donated computer equipment and redistributes it to students, learners, and communities without reliable access to technology. We believe functional hardware shouldn't end up in landfills when it could end up in hands.

What We Do

We operate a full-cycle technology reclamation program. When schools, businesses, or organizations retire computer equipment, we coordinate pickup, securely wipe all data using DOD-standard protocols, perform diagnostics and repairs, install appropriate operating systems, and redistribute the refurbished equipment to qualified recipients.

Every device that passes through our hands undergoes rigorous testing to ensure it meets basic functionality standards. We install lightweight Linux distributions or properly licensed Windows systems depending on hardware capabilities and recipient needs. Our goal is simple: deliver working technology that people can rely on.

Why This Matters

The digital divide isn't just about who owns the newest technology. It's about who has access to any technology at all. In 2026, basic computer access remains a fundamental barrier to education, employment, and civic participation. A student without a computer at home falls behind in coursework. An adult without internet access struggles to apply for jobs. A senior without digital literacy skills faces isolation.

Meanwhile, e-waste continues to grow as one of the fastest-growing waste streams globally. Business-class laptops that could serve users for years get discarded after short corporate refresh cycles. We're working to solve both problems simultaneously: reducing unnecessary waste while expanding access to essential technology.

Our Core Values

Accessibility

Technology access should not be determined by income. We work to ensure that everyone who needs a computer has a pathway to obtain one.

Sustainability

Functional hardware belongs in use, not in landfills. We extend the useful life of technology through careful refurbishment and redistribution.

Transparency

We operate openly about our processes, partnerships, and limitations. Donors and recipients deserve clear information about what we do and how we do it.

Quality

Every device we distribute must meet functional standards. We don't pass along junk; we provide working tools that people can depend on.

Community

This work succeeds because of partnerships. Schools donate equipment, volunteers contribute time, organizations identify recipients. We're stronger together.

Pragmatism

We focus on what works. No unnecessary bureaucracy, no mission creep. We receive equipment, refurbish it, and get it to people who need it.

How We Operate

CDAC is a volunteer-run initiative. We are not a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit, which means we operate with minimal overhead and maximum flexibility. All equipment donations are accepted as-is, and we cannot provide tax receipts. What we can provide is documentation of your donation for your internal records and the satisfaction of knowing your retired equipment is going to people who need it.

We maintain partnerships with local recycling facilities to ensure that equipment we cannot refurbish is properly recycled rather than landfilled. Our data security protocols meet Department of Defense standards for drive sanitization. We take donor privacy and recipient dignity seriously.

Volunteer-Operated

This initiative runs on donated time, donated equipment, and community support. We keep costs minimal and impact maximal. If you're interested in volunteering with equipment pickup, refurbishment, or distribution, please reach out.
